Read aloud
“Technology has transformed almost every aspect of human life over the last few decades. From communication and education to healthcare and entertainment, modern innovations have changed how we live, work, and connect with others. Smartphones, the internet, and artificial intelligence have made information more accessible than ever, allowing people to learn and communicate at a global scale. One of the most significant impacts is in communication. Social media platforms, instant messaging apps, and video calls have revolutionized how we maintain relationships. People can now interact in real-time across continents, creating opportunities for collaboration and cultural exchange. However, these conveniences come with challenges, including over-dependence on devices, reduced face-to-face interactions, and privacy concerns. In education, technology offers flexible learning opportunities. Students can access online courses, interactive tutorials, and virtual libraries, making self-paced learning a reality. Teachers can utilize digital tools to enhance lessons and assess students more effectively. Yet, the digital divide remains a problem: not all students have equal access to devices or stable internet, which can widen inequalities. Healthcare has also benefited from technological advances. Telemedicine, electronic medical records, and AI diagnostics enable faster and more accurate patient care. Patients can consult specialists remotely, and doctors can monitor health conditions in real-time. Despite these advantages, ethical questions arise regarding data security, consent, and potential over-reliance on machines. Finally, technology has changed leisure and entertainment. Streaming platforms, online gaming, and virtual reality experiences provide immersive ways to relax and socialize. While these options enhance lifestyle and creativity, excessive screen time can lead to sedentary habits and mental health concerns. In conclusion, technology is a double-edged sword. It offers immense benefits but also requires careful, responsible use. Understanding both its potential and its risks allows individuals to maximize its advantages while minimizing negative effects.”
Vocabulary
  • persistence – continuing despite difficulty
  • momentum – force that keeps something moving
  • consistency – doing the same thing regularly
  • exposure – contact with something (e.g. language)
  • discipline – controlled behaviour to achieve a goal
